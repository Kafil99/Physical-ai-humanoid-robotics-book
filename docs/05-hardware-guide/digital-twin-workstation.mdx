## Building a Workstation for Digital Twin and Simulation

Developing Physical AI applications often requires significant computational resources, especially when dealing with high-fidelity simulations like NVIDIA Isaac Sim. A powerful **Digital Twin Workstation** is the backbone of efficient robotics development, allowing you to iterate quickly, train complex models, and visualize intricate simulations.

This chapter details the hardware specifications and essential software configurations for building such a workstation.

### Hardware Recommendations

The key component for a robust digital twin workstation is a powerful GPU, but other components are also critical for a smooth development experience.

#### 1. Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) - NVIDIA RTX Series

<Admonition type="danger" title="NVIDIA RTX GPU Required">
NVIDIA Isaac Sim, a core tool in this book, **mandates** an NVIDIA RTX GPU for its real-time ray tracing and simulation capabilities. Integrated graphics or non-NVIDIA GPUs will NOT work for Isaac Sim.
</Admonition>

-   **Recommended**: NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4070 Ti, RTX 4080, RTX 4090, or professional-grade NVIDIA RTX A-series cards.
-   **Minimum**: NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3070 or equivalent.
-   **VRAM**: 12 GB VRAM minimum, 16 GB+ highly recommended, especially for complex scenes or large AI models.

#### 2. Central Processing Unit (CPU)

While the GPU handles most of the heavy lifting for simulation and AI, a strong CPU is important for compilation, running many ROS 2 nodes, and overall system responsiveness.

-   **Recommended**: Intel Core i7 (12th Gen or newer) / i9, or AMD Ryzen 7 (5000 series or newer) / Ryzen 9.
-   **Cores/Threads**: 8 cores / 16 threads or more is beneficial.
-   **Clock Speed**: High single-core performance is still valuable for many tasks.

#### 3. Random Access Memory (RAM)

Simulation environments and IDEs can consume a lot of memory.

-   **Minimum**: 32 GB DDR4/DDR5 RAM.
-   **Recommended**: 64 GB DDR4/DDR5 RAM.
-   **Speed**: Faster RAM (e.g., 3200MHz+ DDR4, 5600MHz+ DDR5) will improve overall system performance.

#### 4. Storage

Fast storage significantly reduces load times for large simulation assets and improves overall system responsiveness.

-   **Primary Drive**: 1 TB NVMe SSD (PCIe Gen4 or Gen5 recommended). This should house your operating system, Isaac Sim installation, and project files.
-   **Secondary Drive (Optional)**: A larger SATA SSD or HDD for less frequently accessed data or backups.

#### 5. Power Supply Unit (PSU)

Ensure your PSU can handle the power demands of your chosen GPU and other components.

-   **Recommended**: 750W to 1000W 80 PLUS Gold or Platinum rated PSU, depending on your GPU.

### Software Configuration

A properly configured software environment is just as important as the hardware.

#### 1. Operating System - Ubuntu 22.04 LTS

-   As detailed in the **Workstation Setup** chapter, **Ubuntu 22.04 LTS** is the recommended operating system for compatibility with ROS 2 and NVIDIA tools.
-   Perform a clean installation on your primary NVMe SSD.

#### 2. NVIDIA Drivers and CUDA Toolkit

-   Install the latest **proprietary NVIDIA drivers** for your GPU. These are typically available through Ubuntu's "Additional Drivers" utility or directly from NVIDIA's website.
-   The **CUDA Toolkit** (which includes CUDA libraries, development tools, and a runtime) will be installed as part of the NVIDIA SDKs (e.g., with Isaac Sim or through `apt`). Ensure it's correctly configured and matches the requirements of your AI frameworks.

#### 3. Docker and NVIDIA Container Toolkit

-   Set up **Docker** and the **NVIDIA Container Toolkit** as described in the **Workstation Setup** chapter. This is crucial for running many ROS 2 and Isaac ROS packages in isolated, GPU-accelerated containers.

#### 4. Development Tools

-   **ROS 2**: Install ROS 2 Humble or Iron, as covered in the **Workstation Setup** chapter.
-   **NVIDIA Isaac Sim**: Install via the Omniverse Launcher.
-   **Visual Studio Code**: The recommended IDE, configured with extensions for Python, C++, ROS, and Docker.

### Verification

After setting up your workstation, perform these checks:

1.  **NVIDIA Driver**: `nvidia-smi` should display your GPU information.
2.  **CUDA**: Run a CUDA sample (e.g., `cuda-install-samples-11-8.sh` then `make` and `./vectorAdd`) to confirm CUDA functionality.
3.  **Docker GPU Access**: `docker run --rm --gpus all nvidia/cuda:12.1.1-base-ubuntu22.04 nvidia-smi` should work.
4.  **ROS 2 Installation**: `ros2 run demo_nodes_cpp talker` and `ros2 run demo_nodes_py listener` should communicate.
5.  **Isaac Sim Launch**: Launch Isaac Sim from the Omniverse Launcher and confirm it opens correctly.

A well-equipped and configured workstation will provide a smooth and efficient development experience, allowing you to focus on the challenging and rewarding aspects of Physical AI and humanoid robotics.
